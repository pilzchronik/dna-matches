1. Background

The Pilz Chronicle documents the Pilz family over centuries based on written sources. One line leads to the Jewish community of Košová Hora (Amschelberg) in Bohemia: Theresia, Kekulé No. 69, converted from Judaism to Catholicism in 1768. Nothing was previously known about her Jewish family.

An autosomal DNA test (FamilyTreeDNA, Kit 330595) produced a strong match with the Zeisl family from Vienna for years. This match remained unprocessed for a long time. Only in 2026 did systematic analysis begin: On GEDmatch, Wolfgang Pilz and Walter Steven Zeisl share 43 common matches. A single segment of 29.1 cM on Chromosome 9 confirms a real genealogical connection — not endogamy noise.

The Core Puzzle: How are the Pilz and Zeisl families related? According to MRCA calculation (4.3 generations), the common ancestor likely lived between 1830 and 1870. The connection probably runs through relatives of Theresia who remained in Bohemia's Jewish marriage network after her conversion in 1768.

Endogamy Hypothesis Refuted: A contiguous segment of 29.1 cM with 4,843 SNPs is well above the threshold for genuine kinship (from about 15 cM). Endogamy-related matches typically consist of many small segments (3–7 cM). Here we have a single large segment — Identical by Descent (IBD), not population noise.

Y-DNA of the Zeisl Family — A Conversion Finding

Research by Zeisl descendants revealed another remarkable finding: Walter Steven Zeisl's Y-DNA shows a match with Peter Caizl in Brno, whose line traces back to a Zeisel who was already Catholic by the mid-18th century. Since the Y-DNA line is otherwise consistent with a deep Jewish background, a conversion from Judaism to Christianity must have taken place at that time.

Significance for surname research: This finding proves that the surname Zeisel already existed as a Jewish name before the Tolerance Edict of Emperor Joseph II (1782) mandated the adoption of fixed surnames. A conversion in the mid-18th century falls in the same period as Theresia/Pessel's conversion from Judaism to Catholicism in 1768 — two independent conversions in the same Bohemian-Moravian region.

The closest geographical reference point of the Zeisl family to Košová Hora is Koloděje nad Lužnicí (cf. Places of Origin, Section 7: Stampfer/Lewy family).

7. Places of Origin of Zeisl Ancestors

The Familiants Laws (Familiantengesetze, 1726–1849) forced marriages between Jewish communities in Bohemia and Moravia. Family heads were allowed to marry only one child (usually the eldest son) in their ancestral community; other sons and daughters had to emigrate. This led to large-scale marriage networks.

9. Excursus: Connection to Arnold Schönberg

           

Erich Zeisl's daughter Barbara married Ronald Schönberg, son of composer Arnold Schönberg (1874–1951, founder of twelve-tone technique). Their son is E. Randol Schönberg, the internationally recognized attorney in the Klimt case ("Woman in Gold"). Randol Schönberg is active on Geni.com and co-founder of JewishGen Austria-Czech SIG.

           
                Erich Zeisl (1905–1959): The composer emigrated in 1938, first to London, then to Los Angeles. His work Requiem Ebraico is a classic of Jewish exile music. Archive: Exilarte.            
       

In April 2026, the family confirmed their willingness to collaborate on researching the shared connection. A comparison of family trees is in preparation.

           

10. Excursus: Hermine Dannhauser née Zeisl — Innsbruck

           

Hermine Dannhauser (née Zeisl, *1875 Jungbunzlau / Juniper) and her daughter Philipine Dannhauser (*1903 Innsbruck) plus other family members were murdered in 1941 from Innsbruck to Sobibor.

           

The Dannhauser family was part of Innsbruck's Jewish community. Witness: Berta Dannhauser (98 years old) survived Kristallnacht on November 9, 1938.

           

Research Note: This connection is a coincidence (the researcher lives in Innsbruck), not directly genealogically connected to the Pilz-Zeisl line. However, it illustrates the fate of the Bohemian Jewish diaspora.
