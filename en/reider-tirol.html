6a. A second strand: the maternal Reider line (Tyrol)

The rest of this page follows the paternal Bechinie/Theresia strand with its Ashkenazi connections. The MyHeritage data from June 2026 additionally brought to light a match from the maternal line — to Tyrol, in the Hochpustertal. It does not belong to the Zeisl cluster and is genetically separate from it; a comparison of shared matches shows no overlap.

The match (anonymized: G. K., b. 1983, South Tyrol) first stood out only through the name Reider and could be established via her family tree as a fourth cousin once removed. The common ancestor is Franz Reider zu Äußerst (b. 1783 Sexten, d. 1857 Kartitsch) ∞ Anna Kofler. The bridge runs through the sibling pair Leonhard Michael Reider (b. 1813, Wolfgang's line) and his sister Anna Reider (b. 1814), who married into the Innerkofler family “vom Pfeifhof”.

Historical territory: Sexten (Italian Sesto) belonged to Austrian Tyrol until 1919 and to Italy/South Tyrol thereafter — which is why the match appears as “from Italy” yet shares the Tyrolean Reider ancestry. Kartitsch and Obertilliach lie in East Tyrol (Lienz district).
