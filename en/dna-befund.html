Y-DNA of the Zeisl Family — A Conversion Finding

Research by Zeisl descendants revealed another remarkable finding: Walter Steven Zeisl's Y-DNA shows a match with Peter C. in Brno, whose line traces back to a Zeisel who was already Catholic by the mid-18th century. Since the Y-DNA line is otherwise consistent with a deep Jewish background, a conversion from Judaism to Christianity must have taken place at that time.

Significance for surname research: This finding proves that the surname Zeisel already existed as a Jewish name before the Tolerance Edict of Emperor Joseph II (1782) mandated the adoption of fixed surnames. A conversion in the mid-18th century falls in the same period as Theresia's conversion from Judaism to Catholicism in 1768 — two independent conversions in the same Bohemian-Moravian region.

The closest geographical reference point of the Zeisl family to Košová Hora was long considered to be Koloděje nad Lužnicí (cf. Places of Origin, Section 7: Stampfer/Lewy family). Since the discovery that a Zeisl family is also documented in Miskowitz (Myslkovice, ~45 km southeast), this location now takes precedence as the nearest reference point (cf. Places of Origin, Section 7).

Note on the place assignment Miskowitz/Myslkovice: The triangulations and the Lazarus pseudo-kits demonstrate that I share a common ancestor with Walter Steven Zeisl 5–8 generations back. The location of that ancestor cannot be read directly from the DNA. The assignment to Miskowitz/Myslkovice rests on historical indicators (Regina Stern *1786 Myslkovice, Seligmann Fürth *1784 Sušice, Zeisl line Rozsochy, Stern/Fürth/Amschelberg nexus). All these places lie within the South Bohemian triangle — so the region is plausible, but the specific location could still shift as new findings emerge. “Myslkovice ancestor” is a working hypothesis, not a proven place assignment.

5. Triangulation Cluster — Ancestral Lines

Triangulation is a method by which multiple relatives share the same DNA segment, pointing to a common ancestor. Based on the GEDmatch Tier-1 triangulation run of April 12, 2026 (12,136 rows, source kit — (Walter Steven Zeisl)) and the Lazarus reconstructions of April 19, 2026, the 43 common matches Pilz↔Z. separate into several independent ancestral lines plus a distinct large-segment cluster on Chromosome 9. Alongside the three earlier lines (A petctdoc cluster, B Gold/Nathanson, C Kieserman — shelved), a further, fourth line has now been confirmed on Chromosome 2 (201–207 Mb), which I share with petctdoc and Renee Fisher (without Walter Steven Zeisl) and which has been externally corroborated by a new match (Joaquin R., Run D) — see Section 5.2.
