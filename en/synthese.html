DNA Overall Synthesis Across All Platforms

Wolfgang Pilz · MyHeritage · AncestryDNA · FamilyTreeDNA · GEDmatch · Status: 13 June 2026 · Addenda 17 + 25 June 2026 · Umbrella document for the four individual analyses

This page brings the four separate platform analyses (MyHeritage, AncestryDNA, FamilyTreeDNA, GEDmatch) together into a single picture. It names the confirmed lines of descent, the still-open top matches and the methodological lessons — and serves as an entry point to the four individual documents.

Core message in one sentence: All four platforms independently confirm the same basic picture — a confirmed maternal Tyrolean line (Reider), a confirmed paternal Bohemian line (Bechinie) and a small, clearly localisable Jewish branch (Theresia → Zeisl) — and they complement one another where any single platform is blind.

Why several platforms? Each database contains different people and different tools. The Tyrolean relatives test on MyHeritage and Ancestry, hardly at all on FTDNA. The Jewish relatives are densely represented on FTDNA and GEDmatch. Y-DNA and mtDNA exist only at FTDNA. Precise segment comparisons and reconstructions only at GEDmatch. Data source and privacy: All DNA matches named here are publicly visible to logged-in users on the respective platforms and are reproduced for research and documentation purposes (see Privacy).

Maternal: Reider (East Tyrol–South Tyrol)

The strongest match on MyHeritage (Daniela Webhofer, 94.7 cM) and on Ancestry (her sister Ursula Webhofer, 123 cM) belong to the same family — a double confirmation through two sisters. Relationship 2nd cousins, common ancestor Leonhard Reider zu Peterer (*1854). This also explains the high “Italian/Slovenian” share of the ethnicity estimates — the genetic cluster of the Alpine southern side, not Italian or Slovenian ancestry.

Paternal: Bechinie (Bohemia)

Oliver Bechinie von Lazan (MyHeritage, 44.9 cM) is a proven cousin through the Bohemian Bechinie line (MRCA Josef Bechinie *1776). Through him the dense Ashkenazi circle of relatives is explained (Theresia, Josef's Jewish convert mother).

Addendum 17 June 2026 (working hypothesis): Alongside Bechinie, a second paternal lead is emerging on Ancestry — a Bartl-Bohemia line. The Ancestry top match Cris Haas (98 cM) clusters with DeanGVaeth, whose tree carries a Josef Bartl *1875 Bohemia; “Bartl” is one of my paternal family names (one of several paternal lines). Still unconfirmed (a common name, to be verified via concrete persons/places) — details on the Ancestry page, Addendum 6.5.

Jewish: Theresia/Pessel → Zeisl

The genetic anchor lies on GEDmatch: Walter Steven Zeisl, 36.57 cM with a 29.1-cM segment on chromosome 9 (triangulation group TG9_0 with Robert Cohen and Jacqueline Jovings). On FTDNA the same segment appears (27.4 cM). The line goes back to Theresia/Pessel from Myslkovice (conversion 1768). The 1.5–4 % Jewish ethnicity share across all platforms is entirely consistent with a single ancestor ~6 generations back (Kekulé 69).

Erzgebirge/Kreissl: non-Ashkenazi line of its own (working hypothesis)

Only FTDNA's Chromosome Browser makes cluster E visible (Chr 13): H. J. (R-L2, 32 cM) and B. Solbrig (with “Kreissl, Czechoslovakia” in the Ancestral Surnames) point to the paternal Erzgebirge line (Kreissl). Not yet proven at segment level.

5. Methodological Lessons

  • Segment beats cM total: A large single segment (Zeisl 29.1; Webhofer 26.5) proves a real relationship. Many small segments are Ashkenazi endogamy noise.
  • Endogamy distorts relationship estimates: For Jewish matches the degrees shown by the platforms are systematically too close. The MRCA may lie centuries back.
  • Ancestry's SideView is unreliable: demonstrably wrong for this kit (Webhofer first assigned paternal instead of maternal) — never use it as proof.
  • FTDNA myOrigins v3 is coarse: the “British” 40 % is an artefact. Read ethnicity percentages across platforms, never in absolute terms.
  • Exact comparisons yield documented figures: GEDmatch One-to-One gives reproducible cM values (Zeisl 36.57; Myslkovice ancestor 108.4) rather than rough estimates.
